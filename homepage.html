Understanding Harassment

Harassment is a harmful behavior where someone intentionally bothers, scares, or hurts another person repeatedly. This can take many forms, including saying mean or hurtful words, sending unwanted messages or pictures, touching or hitting someone without their consent, following or watching someone without their permission, or spreading rumors or lies about them... Types of Harassment

Verbal Harassment: Includes name-calling, insults, and threats. It can erode self-esteem and lead to emotional distress.

Physical Harassment: Involves any unwanted physical contact, such as hitting, pushing, or inappropriate touching.

Emotional Harassment: Encompasses manipulation, isolation, and intimidation tactics designed to control or harm someone psychologically. See More

Legal Information for Victims of Harassment

Know Your Rights

As a victim of harassment, it's crucial to understand your legal rights and the resources available to you. Laws vary by jurisdiction, but there are common protections in place to safeguard individuals from harassment... Legal Protections

Restraining Orders: Victims can apply for restraining orders or protection orders to legally prevent the harasser from contacting or approaching them.

- Title IX of the Education Amendments (1972): Prohibits sex-based discrimination, including sexual harassment and assault.

- Title VI of the Civil Rights Act (1964): Prohibits discrimination based on race, color, or national origin.

- Americans with Disabilities Act (1990): Prohibits discrimination against students with disabilities.
- University policies: universities have their own policies and codes of conduct that prohibit harassment and outline procedures for reporting and addressing incidents. See More
